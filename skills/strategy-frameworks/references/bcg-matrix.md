# BCG Matrix Reference

Detailed methodology for portfolio analysis using the Growth-Share Matrix.

## Overview

The BCG Matrix (Boston Consulting Group Matrix) is a portfolio planning tool that classifies business units or products based on market growth rate and relative market share. It helps allocate resources across a portfolio.

## The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Growth | Share | Cash Flow | Strategy |
|----------|--------|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Stars** | High | High | Neutral | Invest to maintain |
| **Cash Cows** | Low | High | Positive | Harvest |
| **Question Marks** | High | Low | Negative | Invest or divest |
| **Dogs** | Low | Low | Neutral/Negative | Divest |

## Quadrant Details

### Stars (High Growth, High Share)

- Market leaders in growing markets
- Require heavy investment to maintain position
- Eventually become Cash Cows as market matures
- Strategic priority: Invest for growth

### Cash Cows (Low Growth, High Share)

- Market leaders in mature markets
- Generate excess cash with minimal investment
- Fund Stars and Question Marks
- Strategic priority: Maximize cash generation

### Question Marks (High Growth, Low Share)

- Weak position in growing markets
- Require significant investment to gain share
- Binary outcome: become Stars or Dogs
- Strategic priority: Selective investment or exit

### Dogs (Low Growth, Low Share)

- Weak position in mature markets
- Break-even or cash drain
- May have strategic value (defense, synergy)
- Strategic priority: Divest or minimize investment

## Measurement

### Market Growth Rate

- Industry growth rate (not company growth)
- High: typically >10% annually
- Dividing line can be adjusted to context

### Relative Market Share

- Your share ÷ Largest competitor's share
- High: >1.0 (you are the leader)
- Low: <1.0 (competitor is larger)

### Circle Size (Optional)

- Represent revenue or contribution
- Larger circles = bigger business

## Strategic Implications

### Investment Strategy

| From | To | Flow |
|------|----|------|
| Cash Cows | Stars | Fund growth |
| Cash Cows | Question Marks | Selective bets |
| Dogs | Exit | Free up resources |

### Portfolio Balance

A healthy portfolio has:
- Cash Cows generating funding
- Stars for future leadership
- Question Marks as options
- Minimal Dogs

## Limitations

| Limitation | Consideration |
|------------|---------------|
| Only two dimensions | Other factors matter |
| Market definition critical | Wrong definition, wrong analysis |
| Assumes market share = profitability | Not always true |
| Static snapshot | Markets change |
| Growth not always good | Quality of growth matters |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Wrong market definition | Misleading positions | Define carefully |
| Ignoring synergies | Divest valuable Dogs | Consider strategic value |
| Underinvesting in Stars | Lose leadership | Match investment to position |
| Too many Question Marks | Resource dilution | Make clear choices |
